Integrating Technology into Environments & The Importance of Side Projects

Ben McChesney | 10:00am - 11:30am | Booth 1440

The world of digital interaction beyond the web browser has drastically evolved. Ben will go through some of the challenges he and others at his company have faced implementing new technologies like depth cameras, augmented reality, and large touch screens in permanent and unforgiving environments. He will go through Helios' process of creating successful experiences and some pitfalls to avoid mid project. He will also talk about a larger industry issue : making time for side projects.

Camouflage Theory and the Art of Private Branding

Greg Smiley | 10:00am - 11:30am | Booth 3471

In a hands on design exercise, exploree methods to vary forms, visually change the proportion of designs and support multiple brand themes using common or similar platforms and components

If They Don’t Take the Leap, Push Them.

Red Paper Heart | 10:00am - 11:30am | Booth 1400

How do you make someone stop for a moment to consider their own heartbeat? How do you get a crowd of swanky socialites to jump into a pool? Can you measure happiness with a webcam? Sometimes half of the challenge of an installation is getting people to overcome their inhibitions, and give in to enjoying themselves. In this session, Red Paper Heart will teach you all about how they create experiences for the physical world, what they’ve learned, what they’ve had to unlearn, and where they hope to take things next.

Physical to Digital and Back Again

Jordan Pelovitz | 10:00am - 11:30am | Booth 3310

Product development in plugged-in world. An introduction to the world of 3D scanning and 3D printing, with an overview of the current market, technologies, and demonstrations of design and prototyping techniques using these technologies
